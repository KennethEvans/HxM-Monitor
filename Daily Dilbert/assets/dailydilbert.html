Daily Dilbert

This application displays Dilbert comic strips from dilbert.com. It is a specialized browser in that respect and does nothing that cannot be done with a browser. It may stop working if the way the strips are stored at dilbert.com changes. It is for your personal use only, and the usage and images are subject to the rights and restrictions that may be found at that site.

Levels of Interaction

Levels allow you to see the strip at different sizes. Tapping allows you to navigate between strips and levels. The explanations below may seem complicated, but the usage should be simple and intuitive once you try it.

There are three levels of interaction. You can navigate between levels by tapping on the horizontal center of the screen as described below. The last used level is remembered the next time the application is started or resumed.

Level 1

The strip is sized to fit the screen and centered. You cannot zoom or move it. Swiping to the right goes to the previous strip, and swiping to the left goes to the next strip. This is probably the most desirable level unless you have trouble reading the strip.

Level 2

The strip is sized to fit the screen vertically or as large as possible while making at least one third of the strip visible. You can pan horizontally by dragging to read the strip. Swiping does not go to another strip (since it cannot readily be distinguished from panning). Tapping to the right or left is the easiest way to go to another strip in any level. (See below.)

Level 3

You can zoom in and out with two fingers and pan by dragging in any direction. When coming from another level, the zoom and pan state is not changed from that level. When starting at Level 3, the strip is centered on the screen at its default size. Swiping does not go to another strip for the same reasons as discussed above.

Single and Double Tapping

Tapping has the following effects depending on where horizontally on the screen you tap. The taps must also be in the middle third vertically to count. You can thus, for example, keep the device from timing out and shutting off without causing something else to happen by tapping above or below the middle third. Single or double tapping in the center wraps. If you are at Level 3 and single tap, you will go to Level 1. If you are at Level 1 and double tap, you will go to Level 3. Tapping is the most convenient way to navigate between strips and between levels.

Getting New Strips

The application caches the last 5 strips on the SD card if there is one. Otherwise no strips are saved, and each new one must loaded from the web. There is a progress bar when getting strips from the web. It can be cancelled with the back button, but if you do it too near the time it finishes, pushing the back button may back out of the program instead.

Menu Items

Next, Prev, First, Today

Get the next, previous, first, or today's strip. The first strip corresponds to May 16, 1989.

Date

Get the strip for a date you select.

Save

Save a PNG image of the current strip on the SD card. The images will be found in the Dilbert directory on the SD card. Does nothing if there is no SD card.

Share

Share this image via one of the applications on the device that is capable of sharing, such as email. You can choose the application from a list if there is more than one. The file to be used is saved internally and deleted when Daily Dilbert finishes to help keep storage low. It may thus not be available if the other application does not use it before Daily Dilbert finishes.
